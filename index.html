Shutters

The Shutters Project is an interactive art installation designed to engage its users dynamically. As users move within its vicinity, the shutters respond accordingly: opening upon detecting movement and closing when motion subsides. For this project, I assumed responsibility for both firmware and software development. Utilizing OpenCV in Python, I engineered the motion detection software, enabling precise responsiveness to user movements. Complementing this, I leveraged C++ to manage the intricate motor controls essential for the shutters' operation. Additionally, I oversaw the seamless integration of all software and firmware components, ensuring the project's cohesive functionality.
